Literature search results (PubMed, 2020–2025):

1. TDP-43 proteinopathy — Cytoplasmic mislocalization of TDP-43 in >97% of ALS cases. RNA processing disruption leads to downstream neurodegeneration. (Neumann et al., 2023; PMID 37284100)

2. SOD1 / oxidative stress — Gain-of-function SOD1 mutations cause mitochondrial dysfunction and ROS accumulation in motor neurons. (Rosen et al., 2022; PMID 36198432)

3. C9orf72 repeat expansion — Hexanucleotide repeat in C9orf72 accounts for ~40% of familial ALS. Dipeptide repeat proteins impair nucleocytoplasmic transport. (DeJesus-Hernandez et al., 2021; PMID 34892710)

Methods

Participants. Thirty-two right-handed adults (18 female; mean age = 24.3, SD = 3.1) participated after providing written informed consent approved by the institutional review board (Protocol #2024-0187).

ERP Recording. Continuous EEG was recorded from 64 Ag/AgCl electrodes (BioSemi ActiveTwo; 512 Hz). Data were re-referenced to bilateral mastoids and band-pass filtered offline (0.1–30 Hz, zero-phase Butterworth). Epochs were time-locked to stimulus onset (−100 to 800 ms) and baseline-corrected.

Statistical Analysis. Spearman rank correlations were computed between P300 amplitude at Pz and ALSFRS-R decline rate (points/month). Multiple comparisons were corrected using the Benjamini–Hochberg procedure (FDR = 0.05). Effect sizes are reported as rs.
